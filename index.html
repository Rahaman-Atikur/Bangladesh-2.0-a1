Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a renowned Bangladeshi economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is best known for founding Grameen Bank, which pioneered the concept of microfinance—providing small loans to impoverished people, especially women, without requiring collateral. This innovative approach helped millions escape poverty and became a global model for economic development.
